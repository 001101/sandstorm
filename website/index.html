Sandstorm

Personal Cloud Sandbox

The Web
is Broken

When you use a web app today, you usually connect to its developers' servers. This is backwards. Instead, your apps should run on your server.

Sandstorm makes it easy to have your own personal cloud server. Install, use, and share web apps and data through a simple web interface.

Sandstorm
Fixes It

All Your Data in One Place

Why should your personal data be scattered around the internet, stored on computers belonging to dozens of companies? Keep it all in one place.

No Disappearing Apps

Apps running on your server do not disappear when the developer stops supporting them.

No Spying / Data Mining

Sandstorm apps can only talk to the outside world with your explicit permission. That means they can't secretly spy on you, for advertising purposes or otherwise.

End Walled Gardens

Your apps shouldn't only integrate with other apps from the same developer. They should integrate with whatever apps you choose to use. Sandstorm makes it easy.

Stay Secure

Some developers are bad at security. With apps running in a secure sandbox on a server you control, you can defend yourself against poorly-written apps — and even malicious ones.

But what's in it for the developers?

Zero Operational Costs

You don't have to pay for servers nor carry a pager. Just make your app work, put it on the app store, and relax.

Just One Machine

Since every end user is running their own instance of your app, you never need to worry about scaling. You can pretend your app only runs on a single machine.

Security is Built-in

You don't have to implement login. You don't even have to implement sharing — Sandstorm does that for you. You can even export APIs and integrate with other apps without thinking about OAuth.

Any Tech Stack

Sandstorm is a native-code sandbox. It supports any Linux-based technology stack. Use whatever language you prefer.

Level Playing Field

Because Sandstorm handles security, users can safely use apps from developers they don't trust. This means new, small developers can break into the market more easily.

So where do I get it?

Use Our Servers...

We offer managed Sandstorm instances. As of March 2014, this service is in closed alpha testing. As we ramp up, we will send invites to people on the mailing list on a first-come-first-serve basis — sign up below!

...Or Run Your Own

Sandstorm is Open Source Software. You can find the code and installation instructions at Github. You will need a Linux machine and an understanding of Linux system administration.
